# 🧭 1. Vision Document

**Product Name (working):** Personal Health Feedback App (PHFA)

## 🎯 1.1 Purpose

The goal of PHFA is to **help individuals successfully start and sustain intermittent fasting (IF)** by providing:

*   Continuous **personal health feedback**
*   Clear **cause-and-effect insights**
*   Data-driven **motivation and projections**
*   Adaptive, personalized **guidance**

This is not just a fasting timer or habit tracker—  
it is a **feedback-driven behavioral change system**.

***

## 🧠 1.2 Core Problem

Most people:

*   Do not act until they see **health warning signals**
*   Struggle to **start** intermittent fasting
*   Struggle even more to **sustain it**

Key challenges:

*   Results are **slow and inconsistent**
*   Feedback is **unclear or delayed**
*   People cannot see **progress trends**
*   Motivation drops quickly
*   No clear understanding of:
    *   *“Is this working for me?”*
    *   *“When will I see results?”*

***

## 💡 1.3 Vision Statement

> Enable individuals to **see, understand, and trust their own health progress**, so they stay motivated and successfully transform their habits using intermittent fasting.

***

## 🔑 1.4 Core Principles

### 1. Feedback over Tracking

Most apps track data.  
PHFA focuses on **interpreting and explaining it**.

***

### 2. Trends over Raw Data

Users should not care about noisy daily values.

PHFA emphasizes:

*   Moving averages
*   Trend direction
*   Signal strength

***

### 3. Cause → Effect Awareness

Users must clearly see:

*   What actions they took
*   What results those actions produced

Example:

*   “Fasting ≥16h leads to improved energy next day”

***

### 4. Motivation through Evidence

Motivation comes from:

*   Visible improvement
*   Personal data (not generic advice)
*   Predictable outcomes

***

### 5. Personalization over Generic Rules

Each user:

*   Responds differently to fasting
*   Has different progress patterns

The app adapts based on:

*   Their own data
*   Their behavior trends

***

### 6. Simplicity First

*   Minimize cognitive overload
*   Focus on **essential inputs**
*   Avoid feature bloat

***

## 🧩 1.5 Product Positioning

PHFA is **not**:

*   A generic habit tracker
*   A calorie counting app
*   A social fitness platform

PHFA **is**:

*   A **personal health feedback engine**
*   A **motivation system**
*   A **lightweight coaching tool**

***

## 🔍 1.6 Key Differentiators

### ✅ 1. Daily Measurable Feedback

Focus on data users can easily collect at home.

***

### ✅ 2. Insight Generation

*   “Your waist is decreasing at 0.4 cm/week”
*   “Energy improved 15% over 10 days”

***

### ✅ 3. Cause-Effect Correlation

*   Links behavior → outcomes
*   Rare in existing fasting apps

***

### ✅ 4. Goal Projection

*   Predict time to achieve goals
*   Uses real user trends

***

### ✅ 5. Adaptive Guidance

*   Suggests adjustments when:
    *   Progress stalls
    *   Energy drops
    *   Consistency changes

***

### ✅ 6. Motivation Engine

*   Contextual nudges
*   Evidence-based encouragement
*   Progress awareness

***

## ⚠️ 1.7 Constraints & Risks

### Data Limitations

*   User-input data is noisy
*   Must rely on smoothing and trends

***

### Health Accuracy

*   Cannot guarantee outcomes
*   Must present insights probabilistically

***

### Drop-off Risk

*   Early disengagement is common
*   Must show **early meaningful signals**

***

### Overcomplexity

*   Risk of adding too many metrics
*   Must prioritize clarity over completeness

***

## 🧭 1.8 Product Strategy

### Phase 1 — Validation

*   Manual tracking (spreadsheet)
*   Validate insight logic

***

### Phase 2 — MVP

Focus on:

*   Daily input
*   Trend visualization
*   Basic insights

Avoid:

*   AI complexity
*   Social features
*   Over-engineering

***

### Phase 3 — Intelligence Layer

*   Correlation detection
*   Adaptive recommendations
*   Goal prediction

***

### Phase 4 — Refinement

*   Improve usability
*   Expand metrics
*   Introduce optional advanced features

# 📘 2. Use Cases Document

## 👤 Primary User

*   Individuals starting intermittent fasting
*   Motivated by health concerns
*   Struggle with consistency and motivation

***

## 🧭 2.1 Onboarding

### Use Case: Initial Setup

**User wants to define goals and baseline**

Inputs:

*   Weight
*   Waist
*   Optional metrics (BP, body fat, glucose)
*   Goal (e.g., lose 5kg, reduce waist)

Output:

*   Baseline recorded
*   Progress tracking initialized

***

## ⏱️ 2.2 Daily Logging

### Use Case: Record Fasting + Health Signals

User logs daily:

**Tier 1 (required, simple):**

*   Fasting duration
*   Weight
*   Energy level (1–10)
*   Hunger level (1–10)
*   Mood
*   Sleep quality

**Tier 2 (optional):**

*   Body fat %
*   Blood pressure
*   Glucose

***

## 📊 2.3 Progress Monitoring

### Use Case: View Trends

User views:

*   7-day / 14-day averages
*   Trend direction
*   Signal clarity

Examples:

*   “Weight trending down”
*   “Energy stable”

***

## 🔗 2.4 Insight Generation

### Use Case: Understand Cause & Effect

System detects patterns:

Examples:

*   “Longer fast → better energy next day”
*   “Late eating → poorer sleep”

User benefit:

*   Understands what works *for them*

***

## 🎯 2.5 Goal Tracking & Projection

### Use Case: Estimate Goal Achievement

System provides:

*   Estimated time to goal
*   Trend-based projections

Examples:

*   “At current pace, goal reachable in \~6–8 weeks”

***

## 🧠 2.6 Adaptive Guidance

### Use Case: Adjust Strategy

When:

*   Progress stalls
*   Negative signals appear

System suggests:

*   Adjust fasting duration
*   Change meal timing
*   Improve hydration/rest

***

## 🔔 2.7 Motivation & Nudging

### Use Case: Stay Motivated

Examples:

*   “You are close to measurable change”
*   “Consistency improved this week”
*   “Breaking streak delays goal”

***

## 📉 2.8 Plateau Detection

### Use Case: Detect Stagnation

System identifies:

*   No improvement over time

Responds with:

*   Explanation
*   Recommended adjustments

***

## 👥 2.9 Optional Social (Future)

### Use Case: Small Group Sharing

*   Share progress with friends
*   Accountability without public exposure

***

## ⚙️ 2.10 Data Review & Reflection

### Use Case: Periodic Review

User reviews:

*   Weekly summaries
*   Monthly insights

System highlights:

*   What worked
*   What didn’t
*   Suggested next steps

***
